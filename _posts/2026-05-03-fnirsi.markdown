### THIS IS NOT A REVIEW

#### [Jump to teardown part](#teardown-guide-or-some-shit-idk)

About a year and a half ago, I decide to get myself a portable soldering since I was moving to my university dorm at the time and it would be nice to have a type-c powered soldering iron. It's something that I don't think I would use often but would really nice to have when I need it. 

I got the set with all the accessory except for the 65w charger since I have 2 anyways.

I did not expect to like it that much but it has been my main soldering iron even if I travel back home. The fact that I can easily powered it with just a power bank means that I can solder anywhere I want. Plus, 60w type c cables are quite thin (compare to normal irons) means that it is extremely easy to move around. The tips are quite nice as well, I believe they have some sort of coating ? as I notice it doesn't corrode or become dull like my other irons. (Probably a common thing on more expensive irons)

Since I have the full iron set, I even manage to do some micro soldering with it although not successfully but way better than using my old iron. (I warped the connector with the old iron and did not notice it)

fast forward to a few days ago when I use it to put some heat insert into the HDD tray, I plugged it in andddd nothing. 

At first I thought it was my charger since that thing can be picky at times, I pull out my power bank only to discovered that it still works but the display is broken

# Teardown guide or some shit idk

1. Remove the iron tip

2. Pull the rubber grip out

3. Remove the end cap by using something thin

4. Remove the display bezel using the same tool (I found it easier to pry on the top edge)

5. Remove the button and pull the entire thing out from the iron tip entrance(?)

![iron with display bezel, rubber grip and end cap removed](/images/hs-01/dis-1.webp)

![iron fully dissemble](/images/hs-01/dis-full.webp)

The main ic is a CH571F along with CH224K PD trigger IC which from the datasheet it supports BLE ???

![image of main ic with usb pd trigger ic](/images/hs-01/main-ic.webp)

The mosfet seems to be 7409

![image of mosfet](/images/hs-01/mosfet.webp)

as for the display, the best I can find is probably a SSD1316 128x32 14 pin OLED. It contains the following marking

- LT1316P02C
- L2832TSWV
- P241309-18-A024

![image of display ribbon + backside](/images/hs-01/disp-label.webp)

#### ending notes

Overall I would say it's a pretty good soldering iron, especially if you are a beginner. It's nice to have a iron that doesn't required a dedicated area just for it. But the display seems like it is sort of common issue ? maybe idk I saw someone else has the same problem and I do think it's a issue as how tight they bend the ribbon. Another issue I saw other people have is where a power regulator just blew up for no reason at all so QC might be hit or miss. 

Also is is quite funny that a soldering iron that can have firmware update. Hell I actually updated mine lol, idk what they fixed (I think it's something to do with temperature calibration) but it at least good to know the patched out whatever bugs they find (which again is funny as a iron just need to regulate the temperature). As you can see above, I came across IronOS seems like they are a third party firmware for soldering iron. No idea when they would add support for it or what features they have would make someone to install on their iron.
